## What You're Doing Wrong
Have you ever read someones essay and thought to yourself, "Wow the content is great, but they don't know how to format their paragraphs or sentences". If you're not spending the time to organize your thoughts into paragraphs and fix the punctuation of your sentences, you're just wasting your readers time. Stop making run-on sentences and instead use commas and periods to signal a short pause or the end of a thought. Stop making one paragraph a page long and instead split it into smaller paragraphs with similar ideas. Use indents at the start of a paragraph and quotation marks to indicate a phrase someone said. What I'm trying to say is, by following a standardized way of writing, it will make it easy for your readers to follow along. The same can be applied to coding as well and is known as the coding standard. It's basically a set of rules for how your code should be formatted.

## Help Me Fix It!
So now that you understand the importance of having a coding standard, you can start applying it to your code. Now you might be thinking, "Okay I get the importance, how do I go about applying it?". The answer is simple, ESLint. ESLint is a plugin that follows a coding standard that you can apply to your IDE. It will give you errors when your formatting needs correction and even gives you the option to automatically fix it for you. It will save you and everyone else time by making your code easy to read. This is especially useful if you're maintaining a large code base, so that sorting through it wont burn your eyes. Go ahead and give it a try!

## Whats So Great about ESLint?
I personally enjoy having ESLint installed to IntelliJ for writing javascript code. You get a a little x if there's any error that need fixing. It'll highlight the exact line that needs the fixing and offer a dropdown that will let you decide if you want it to fix it for you. These errors could be as simple as not including the correct amount of spacing, missing indentations, or missing semicolons. Once all the errors are gone, you'll recieve a little check mark to let you know everithing is on par with the coding standards. I'm glad I don't have to waste time manually formating my code when I can have ESLint do it for me.
