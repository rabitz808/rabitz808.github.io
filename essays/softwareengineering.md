## Learning to Love 
You may hit a point in time when you feel like you don't know what you're doing or where to start or you may even get past that point and come across a bigger more challenging problem. While it's easy to just give up and forget about everything, in the end you'd be failing yourself. Failing and not giving up is all part of the journey to developing your potential, not just in coding, but also in life. The reason I'm saying this is to let you know you aren't alone and that there are many other people out there going through the same struggles. When it comes to computer science in particular, you have to learn to love the challenge. As long as you put in effort to learn, you wont be failing yourself.

## Hopes and Aspirations
Personally, after completing my degree I hope to get a career in software engineering or web development. I feel as though it's still too early for me to decide exactly what I want to do and which languages to learn, so for now I'm just learning the languages required for my courses and ones for personal hobbies. It's important to go into everything with a goal in mind. Setting goals will make sure you're prepared for the future and is a good way to pace yourself.

## Overall Experiences
So far with the courses I've taken I've learned a little in C, C++, Java, and JavaScript. I prefer learning Java and JavaScript over C and C++, however I do think they're all great languages to learn. Obviously they all have their pros and cons with C and C++ being a little more technical. The biggest challenge for me throughout the years as a student was learning to adopt a different mindset. I've been conditioning myself to think more critically in terms of attacking problems and although I still need more work, the progress so far has been good. Discrete math will always be a struggle for me, but I'm doing my best to learn. Having a challenge can be great for showing growth. In the end, I hope to be able to solve more technical problems without referring so much to notes or lecture materials.
